Spending Styles Explained...

It is important to note that spending personality types are not always one-size-fits-all. Below, we have provided a brief description of the three spending personality types you could receive from our questionnaire. The purpose of our questionnaire is to help you recognize your spending type, and address any challenges that could arise from it. After completing our questionnaire, please utilize our budget calculator considering your spending type!

Saver

Savers are conservative by nature, and don't take big risks with investments or large purchases. Savers fall on a continuum, spanning from those who avoid spending at all costs, to those who generally save but don't shy away from spending on things that are deemed as valuable to them. They typically don't spend money unnecessarily, and look to get good value out of the money they are spending.

Spender

Spenders are more willing to spend money on unnecessary things, as they tend to not worry as much about their financial obligations. Spenders are constnatly looking for good bargains regardless of their financial status. They often get by with little financial literacy, but manage. When it comes to investing, they are comfortable spending money and are willing to take big risks.

Debtor

Debtors typically spend more money than they can earn while not putting much thought into invesments and their financial obligations. They fail to keep tabs on what they spend and where they spend it. Debtors develop emotional satisfaction from spending money. Debtors also fail to take advantage of any bonuses given to them.
